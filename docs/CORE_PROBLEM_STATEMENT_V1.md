# Core Problem Statement v1
## The problem Continuum is built to address

Derived from analysis of 30 AI incident cases across 9 industries.
This is not a product description. It is a problem definition.

---

## The Core Observation

Across 30 cases, the failure was rarely that AI said the wrong thing.

The failure was that AI moved from providing advice
to making decisions on behalf of the organization —
without being authorized to do so.

---

## The Problem in One Sentence

When AI shifts from giving recommendations to bearing organizational risk,
the governance problem begins.

---

## What This Means in Practice

Every organization has decisions that carry risk.
Some of those decisions can be delegated.
Some cannot.

The line between "AI can help here" and "AI cannot decide this" is the authority boundary.

When AI crosses that boundary — even while technically performing its assigned task —
the organization bears consequences it did not authorize.

---

## Five Authority Boundaries That Appear Repeatedly

Based on 30-case research set. Observations only, not general statistics.

| Authority Type | What AI Is Not Authorized To Do | Observed Cases |
|---------------|----------------------------------|----------------|
| Policy Interpretation | Create binding interpretations of company policy | Air Canada, Insurance |
| Financial Commitment | Make commitments that create financial obligations | CRM discount, B2B invoice, Credit |
| Risk Appetite | Decide how much risk the organization will accept | Credit approval, Medical triage, Security |
| Professional Judgment | Make final determinations in licensed domains | Medical, Legal, HR |
| Access Control | Determine what information it is allowed to see | Microsoft Copilot |

---

## The Most Frequently Observed Boundary

Risk Appetite Authority appears across the widest range of industries:
finance, healthcare, cybersecurity, supply chain.

In each case, the question is not whether AI made a mistake.
The question is: who authorized AI to decide how much risk the organization would accept?

---

## What Continuum Is Built For

Continuum does not make AI smarter.

Continuum defines the authority boundaries that AI is not permitted to cross —
and enforces those boundaries in real time,
before the decision reaches the system, the customer, or the court.

---

## The Governance Principle

Organizations have not yet found a reliable way
to show AI where its authority ends.

That is the problem Continuum is built to solve.
